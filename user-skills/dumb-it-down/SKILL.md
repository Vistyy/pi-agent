---

Dumb it down for me.

Treat this as feedback that both the explanation and the idea may be too complex.
First, simplify the idea, solution, or plan itself.
If a simpler approach solves the same problem without losing anything important, use it.
If the complexity is necessary, say why in one sentence.

Then explain the result in plain words.
Give enough context for someone new to the project to understand it.
Keep it short and focused on what I need to understand or decide.

For the rest of this conversation, keep simplifying both the ideas and the language unless I ask for more detail.
